A Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) waiver is a credit policy by which a lender agrees not to charge LMI on a residential mortgage despite the borrower's loan-to-value ratio (LVR) exceeding the standard 80% threshold. For a first home buyer borrowing AUD 600,000 at a 90% LVR, this typically saves between AUD 8,500 and AUD 14,500 in upfront LMI premium. Per APRA's December 2025 quarterly authorised deposit-taking institution data, around 24% of new first home buyer loans written in the September 2025 quarter accessed some form of LMI waiver — either lender-specific professional waivers, the federal Home Guarantee Scheme, or 85% LVR no-LMI programs that have proliferated since 2023. In this piece I map out what each waiver category looks like in 2026, who qualifies, and the trade-offs against alternatives like Genuine Savings First Home Buyer products.

**Data note**: Interest rate, LMI premium and product feature references in this piece are as of April 2026 per each lender's official product pages. Tax and stamp duty rules reflect FY25-26 ATO and state revenue office guidance. Rates and offers change frequently; always confirm with the current state of the lender schedule before acting.

## What a "professional" LMI waiver actually means

A professional LMI waiver applies when a borrower's occupation falls into a list of approved professions and the lender agrees to lend up to 90% LVR (or in some cases 95% LVR) without charging LMI. The borrower still needs to satisfy serviceability (the standard 3% APRA buffer test), credit history, and deposit verification. The waiver removes only the LMI insurance fee — not any other underwriting requirement.

Each lender maintains its own profession list. The list is gated by what the lender considers "high-income, low-default-risk" cohorts. The most commonly waived professions across the major lenders in 2026:

- Medical: Doctors (registered medical practitioners), Dentists, Veterinarians, Pharmacists
- Legal: Solicitors, Barristers
- Accounting: Chartered Accountants, Certified Public Accountants
- Finance: Actuaries, qualified financial planners (CFP)
- Engineering: Mining engineers, mechanical engineers, electrical engineers (often only at certain LVR levels)

Some lenders are aggressive: Macquarie Bank's medical doctor program waives LMI to 95% LVR with no minimum income test. Others are conservative: NAB's professional waiver requires 90% LVR maximum and a minimum gross income of AUD 150,000.

## How LMI waivers stack up vs the federal Home Guarantee Scheme

The First Home Guarantee (FHBG), Regional Home Guarantee (RHG) and Family Home Guarantee (FHG) — collectively the federal Home Guarantee Scheme — provide a separate but parallel path. Under the scheme, eligible first home buyers can purchase with as little as a 5% deposit without paying LMI, with the federal government effectively underwriting the additional risk.

Per Housing Australia FY25-26 guidance, 50,000 places are available across the three guarantees combined. Allocation is split roughly 35,000 / 10,000 / 5,000 between FHBG / RHG / FHG. Eligibility is income-tested (AUD 125,000 single / AUD 200,000 couple), property-value capped (varies by location), and is restricted to first home buyers who have not previously owned property in Australia.

Comparison framework:

| Feature | Professional LMI Waiver | Federal Home Guarantee | 85% LVR No-LMI |
|---|---|---|---|
| Minimum deposit | 10% (90% LVR) or 5% (95% LVR) | 5% | 15% (85% LVR) |
| Income test | Yes (typically AUD 150k+) | Yes (AUD 125k single) | No |
| Profession test | Yes (specific list) | No | No |
| Place cap | None | 50,000 / yr | None |
| Property value cap | Lender's standard policy | State-specific cap | Lender's standard policy |
| First buyer requirement | No (some lenders) | Yes (strict) | No |

A doctor with AUD 220,000 income and AUD 80,000 deposit on an AUD 800,000 purchase has three viable paths: Macquarie's 95% LVR program (10% deposit needed at most), professional waiver at 90% LVR (10% deposit needed), or Home Guarantee Scheme (5% deposit needed if eligible income-wise, but the 50,000-cap rationing applies).

## The 85% LVR No-LMI standard products

Roughly half a dozen lenders in 2026 (ING, Bankwest, ME Bank, plus secondary tiers at the majors) maintain a generic 85% LVR no-LMI product available to any first home buyer. The economic logic is straightforward: LMI premiums above 85% LVR are tightly regulated, and the additional risk for the lender between 80% and 85% is relatively contained. Many lenders absorb that risk in exchange for a slightly higher base interest rate.

Trade-off in numbers:

For a AUD 700,000 purchase, AUD 105,000 deposit (15%), AUD 595,000 borrowed at 85% LVR:
- Standard LMI premium at 85% LVR (without waiver): around AUD 4,200
- LMI savings via no-LMI 85% LVR product: AUD 4,200 upfront
- Variable rate premium for the no-LMI product vs the comparable 80% LVR product: typically 0.05–0.15 percentage points higher
- Cost of 0.10 percentage point higher rate over 5 years on AUD 595,000: around AUD 3,000

In this scenario, the LMI saving roughly offsets 5 years of slightly elevated interest. Beyond 5 years, the no-LMI product becomes more expensive than going LMI-with-LMI-and-a-lower-rate. This is a real trade-off; it doesn't always favour the no-LMI route.

## What about non-listed professions?

Some lenders maintain "case-by-case" assessment rather than fixed profession lists. Bendigo and Bank of Queensland, for example, will entertain LMI waiver applications from non-listed professions for borrowers earning AUD 200,000+ with strong career stability and minimum 12 months in current role.

The application path is:
1. Standard pre-approval submission
2. Loan officer flags the borrower as professional waiver candidate
3. Underwriter reviews income stability, profession context, default risk model
4. Decision returned within 5-10 business days
5. If approved, LMI premium is removed from the loan calculation

Approval rates are roughly 35-50% for non-listed professions in this case-by-case program based on broker network feedback in 2025.

## Common pitfalls for first home buyers exploring LMI waivers

**Pitfall 1: Not checking deposit source rules**

Even with LMI waiver, lenders apply Genuine Savings rules. AUD 30,000 of the deposit must typically be saved in the borrower's own name over 3+ months. Gifted funds or loans from family don't count toward Genuine Savings unless explicitly approved.

**Pitfall 2: Misjudging serviceability**

LMI waiver doesn't relax serviceability assessment. The APRA 3% interest rate buffer still applies. A 90% LVR loan at 6.0% p.a. is still serviced at 9.0% p.a. for assessment. Some borrowers assume LMI waiver creates additional borrowing capacity — it does not.

**Pitfall 3: Stamp duty miscalculation**

LMI waiver only removes LMI. State stamp duty applies as normal. NSW first home buyers can get full stamp duty exemption up to AUD 800,000 property value via FHBAS, which often reduces upfront cost more than LMI waiver.

**Pitfall 4: Not negotiating the rate**

LMI-waived loans aren't always the same rate as standard loans. Some lenders charge a 0.05–0.15 percentage point loading for the waiver. Always check both rate and total upfront cost.

**Pitfall 5: 90% LVR triggers other policy gates**

A 90% LVR loan triggers maximum exposure limits at some lenders. If the borrower already has investment property loans, a new 90% LVR purchase may push them over the lender's maximum exposure limit, even though LMI is waived.

## When NOT to chase an LMI waiver

A professional LMI waiver is genuinely good economics in three scenarios:

1. AUD 100,000+ savings in LMI premium that you'd otherwise pay (i.e., property value AUD 800,000+ at 90% LVR);
2. Serviceability is comfortable (not stretched);
3. You're certain about staying in the property 5+ years.

It's not the right call when:
- You have less than 12 months in your current professional role
- You're stretching serviceability — the 3% buffer at 90% LVR can shock at next interest rate cycle
- You may move within 2-3 years and exit costs (break costs, stamp duty on the next purchase) eat the savings
- The 85% LVR no-LMI alternative offers simpler underwriting and you have a 15% deposit

## FAQ

**Q1: Can a registered nurse access a professional LMI waiver?**

Most major banks do not include registered nurses in their professional waiver list. Some second-tier lenders (Bendigo, Bank of Queensland) will consider on a case-by-case basis. Macquarie Bank's medical waiver is doctor-specific, not nurse-inclusive.

**Q2: I'm a non-resident on a Subclass 482 visa — am I eligible for LMI waiver?**

Most LMI waivers apply only to Australian permanent residents and citizens. Foreign Investment Review Board (FIRB) restrictions also apply, including a 0.5–4% additional foreign buyer surcharge on stamp duty depending on state.

**Q3: How long does the LMI waiver process add to my application?**

Typically 5-10 business days additional underwriting time. The waiver application is submitted alongside the standard loan application; the timing impact is in the underwriter's review queue.

**Q4: If I'm approved for an LMI waiver, can I refinance to a different lender later and keep the waiver?**

The waiver is not transferable. If you refinance to a different lender, you'd need to re-apply at that lender's policy. If your LVR is below 80% by then, LMI doesn't apply anyway. But if you've drawn down equity to >80% LVR via the refinance, you'd need to negotiate the new lender's LMI policy.

**Q5: What's the difference between LMI waiver and LMI capitalisation?**

LMI waiver means you don't pay LMI at all. LMI capitalisation means you pay LMI but it's added to the loan amount and amortised over the loan term (you pay it gradually as part of repayments rather than upfront). Both reduce your initial cash outlay but capitalisation increases your total interest cost over time.

**Q6: Can I combine an 85% LVR no-LMI product with the First Home Guarantee Scheme?**

No. The Home Guarantee Scheme requires participating in the 5% deposit pathway through approved lenders. Most 85% LVR no-LMI products require a 15% deposit. They are separate paths; you choose one or the other.

**Q7: For a stay-at-home parent or partial-income borrower, are LMI waivers realistic?**

Most professional LMI waivers require minimum income tests (AUD 150,000+ joint income at major banks; AUD 200,000+ at Macquarie). A single-income household at AUD 100,000 generally won't qualify. The 85% LVR no-LMI standard products are more accessible.
